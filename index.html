Upsilon Pi Epsilon

Upsilon Pi Epsilon is the International Honor Society for the Computing and Information Disciplines. Founded in 2013, the chapter here at Wash U's primary purpose is to honor academic excellence in the computing and information disciplines. However, we also intend to work with other student groups to organize events to benefit the local computer science community.

We'd like to extend our thanks to Answers.com for their help in starting this chapter.

If you are interested in joining, check out the requirements.

Who We Are

Upsilon Pi Epsilon is a home for undergraduate and graduate students with a strong passion for computing and information fields.

The Missouri Eta chapter here at WashU focuses on identifying academic excellence and fostering a strong computer science community both on campus and around St. Louis. We put on events with other student groups to benefit the local computing community.

The nationwide organization of UPE also provides a strong network of support for members. UPE is endorsed by some of the largest technology organizations including IEEE Computer Society (IEEE-CS) and The International Federation of Engineering Education Societies (IFEES).

History

The first Upsilon Pi Epsilon chapter was founded at Texas A&M in 1967. It was founded to promote the fast-developing fields of computing and information technology and to encourage development of knowledge within these fields.

Then just as now, members were required to have both exceptional talent within computer science as well as a desire for interdisinplinary work and a desire to use their talent to contriubute to society.

How to Apply

In order to hold a high but fair standard, UPE follows a consistent set of membership requirements.

Applicants without the listed requirements will not be considered.

Undergraduate Requirements

  • Sophomore (or greater) class standing
  • Candidate for an undergraduate degree in the computing and information diciplines
  • Completed ≥60 semester hours of undergraduate coursework, including at least 18 in basic courses in the area of the computing and information disciplines
  • GPA of at least 3.5, and at least 3.6 in computer science courses
  • Rank in the top 35% (20% for sophomores) of computing students satisfying the credit requirement

Graduate Requirements

  • Sophomore (or greater) class standing
  • Candidate for a graduate degree in the computing and information disciplines
  • Completed ≥18 semester hours of graduate coursework
  • GPA of at least 3.6, excluding research and seminar courses
Note: Only our faculty adviser will have access to student grades and records. The faculty advisor will notify the Membership Committee if an applicant is eligible or ineligible, but no personal academic details will be shared.

Executive Council

As per the national UPE constitution, our chapter has an executive council comprised of 8 members: the President, a Vice President, a Faculty Advisor, a Secretary, a Treasurer, a Historian, a Webmaster, and an External Relations chair.

President

A senior, Jay is the President of UPE for 2014. He calls and presides at all meetings, appoints committees, and authorizes payment of bills presented against the chapter.

Vice President

Pursuing a master's degree, Gabe is the VP of UPE for 2014. He is able to perform the duties of President in the absence of the President. He will also become President in case of the resignation, impeachment, or disability of the President. In addition, he is responsible for the coordination of committees and the proper conduct of meetings.

Faculty Advisor

A professor and the interim CSE department chair, Dr. Buhler is the Faculty Advisor of UPE. He consults on matters of chapter policy and acts as auditor of chapter finances. He also is the presiding member of the Membership Committee.

Secretary

A senior, Nick is the Secretary of UPE for 2014. He keeps records of all transactions of the chapter. In addition, he is responsible for the handling of correspondence for the chapter. Gabe will notifiy all active members of the date and purpose of special meetings, at least one week prior to the meeting. Lastly, he has the responsibility to oversee the Internal Relations Officer and the External Relations Officer in their responsibilities.

Treasurer

A senior, Yuwen is the Treasurer of UPE for 2014. He collects all dues and assessments and issues, with the assistance of the Secretary, notices of special monetary assignments. He is in charge of charge of all funds of the chapter, and pays therefrom bills presented against the chapter and approved for payment.

Historian

A senior, Jon is the Historian of UPE for 2014. He keeps a systematic account in chronological order of events of the chapter and to keep the minutes of the meetings. The Historian must additionally be familiar with the history of both the Missouri Eta Chapter as well as national history of Upsilon Pi Epsilon. The minutes containing financial statements shall be kept secret from all but the Executive Council.

Webmaster

A senior, Garrett is the Webmaster of UPE for 2014. He maintains and manages the website of the chapter.

External Relations Chair

A senior, Andrew is the External Relations officer of UPE for 2014. He corresponds with organizations outside of the affiliated campus to foster professional relationships and pursue fundraising efforts. He also is responsible for maintaining and distributing the Chapter's resume book.
